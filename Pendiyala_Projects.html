Python Project : Miles the Motivator

Date

January 7, 2021 - February 9, 2021 (33 days)

Class

AP Computer Science Applications (AP CSA)

Members

Kaushik Pendiyala, Akash Dharba, and Kaif Jeelani

Skills Gained from Project

Teamwork and Resiliance

Synopsis of Project

This was a project where my team and I coded a discord bot that casually interacted with users on its server. In this prototype, we focused on giving people motivational advice through stress-relieving games and motivational videos based on search queries. Our bot had 3 main functionalities: Stalker, Game, and Query. The Stalker functionality occurred when the discord bot randomly interjected within conversations and provided relevant information based on the subject being discussed. For example, if two users were talking about Lebron James, our bot would interject the conversation with information about Lebron James because it identified him as the subject of the discussion. The next functionality is the game functionality, where the user can play a simple game of tic-tac-toe with the bot; this is triggered by the command “!play”. And finally, when asked a question, Miles will process it and output links to the three most relevant youtube and google search results. This functionality is triggered through the command “Hey Miles,” followed by the specific question/query. The walkthrough of the functionalities oof the bot can be found in this video. In this project, my specific role was to work on the query functionality of our bot as well as the documentation and presentation. Throughout this project, we implemented various libraries such as spaCy, scrapy, and discord.py. Initially, I was very confused because we worked with Python, which was a language I was not familiar with. However, with the help of my teammates and the internet, I was able to implement different libraries in our bot. An important lesson that I learned from this project was the importance of teamwork and perseverance. Even though I didnt understand the language at first, I did a lot of research on the topic and familiarized myself with it. In addition, because I was new to Python, my team and I had to find an efficient way to delegate tasks. We decided that Akash and I would focus on the presentation and documentation aspect of the project, while Kaif worked on the code. Overall, this project taught me many important skills regarding perseverance and teamwork.

Greenfoot Project : Natural Selection Simulation

Date

June 26, 2020 - July 13, 2020 (18 days)

Class

AP Computer Science Applications (CSA)

Members

Kaushik Pendiyala, Mark Owen Macasaet, and Arin Manohar

Skills Gained from Project

Leadership and Self-Resilience

Synopsis of Project

In this project, my partners and I created a natural selection simulation using snakes as our example. We had 2 types of snakes, one was blue and one was green. Because our background was green, the green snakes had an advantage over the blue snakes because they could camouflage better in the surroundings. We implemented a reproduction feature in which each snake had a probability to reproduce with their own species when they touched. We also introduced a predator (an eagle) to simulate natural selection. Because the green snakes could camouflage better in the surroundings, they had a less probability of being eaten than the blue snakes. Over time, we observed that the blue snakes went extinct while the green snakes were thriving and overpopulating. This video walks through our simulation in detail and goes over the code and you can access our code and play the simulation youself through here. This was our first project using Greenfoot, so we had trouble using the interface. We had to do a lot of research on the Greenfoot interface and learn about Greenfoot as we progressed through the project. Another problem that we encountered was that our whole program broke when we were writing our predator method. To fix this, we re-wrote the predator method and took a different approach that was more feasible and through this experience, we developed good coding practices. This was a project that everyone was very passionate about, as we got together everyday and worked on it resiliently. In this project, my role was the leader because I got the group together and decided on the basis of our game while delegating tasks to my group members. Many of the features in our game were suggested by different people and everything we did was a collective effort. Overall, this was a very fun and interesting project where I learned many important lessons regarding leadership and self-resilience.
